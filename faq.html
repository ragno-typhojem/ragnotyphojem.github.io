Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most common questions about KodKit

General Questions

What is KodKit?

KodKit is a forked version of Scratch 3.0, designed to enhance the original platform with additional features and improvements. It's a block-based coding environment that allows users to create interactive stories, games, and animations without needing to learn complex programming syntax.

Is KodKit free to use?

Yes, KodKit is completely free to use. As an open-source project, we believe in making coding accessible to everyone without any cost barriers.

What platforms does KodKit support?

Currently, KodKit is available for Windows 10 and 11. We're working on expanding support to other platforms like macOS and Linux in future releases.

How does KodKit differ from Scratch?

While KodKit is based on Scratch 3.0, we've added several enhancements including improved performance, additional coding blocks, expanded asset libraries, and better export options. Our goal is to maintain the simplicity of Scratch while adding features that more advanced users might need.

Technical Questions

What are the system requirements for KodKit?

KodKit requires:

  • Windows 10 or 11
  • 4GB RAM (8GB recommended)
  • 1GB free disk space
  • Intel Core i3 or equivalent (Core i5 or better recommended)
  • Internet connection for online features
Can I import my existing Scratch projects into KodKit?

Yes, KodKit supports importing .sb3 files created with Scratch 3.0. Most projects should work without any modifications, though some might require minor adjustments if they use features that have been modified in KodKit.

How do I update KodKit to the latest version?

KodKit includes an auto-update feature that checks for updates when you start the application (if you're connected to the internet). You can also manually check for updates from the Help menu within the application.

Is an internet connection required to use KodKit?

KodKit can be used offline for most features, but an internet connection is required for sharing projects, accessing the online community, and downloading additional assets from our libraries.

Project Questions

How do I save my projects in KodKit?

KodKit automatically saves your work as you go. You can also manually save by clicking on "File" and then "Save" or by pressing Ctrl+S. Projects are saved in the .kk format (KodKit's native format) or can be exported as .sb3 for compatibility with Scratch.

Can I share my KodKit projects online?

Yes, KodKit allows you to share your projects through our online community platform. You can also export your projects as standalone applications or HTML5 web pages that can be hosted on your own website.

Are there limits to project size or complexity?

KodKit has improved performance compared to standard Scratch, allowing for larger and more complex projects. However, extremely large projects with thousands of sprites or scripts may still experience performance issues depending on your computer's specifications.
